# Executive Brief: Cisco ThousandEyes Digital Experience Monitoring Expansion

## Recommendation

Approve the proposed four-year Cisco ThousandEyes renewal, expanding endpoint agent licenses from 1,600 to 13,000 while maintaining our existing infrastructure and network monitoring capabilities.

Although the firm currently owns approximately 1,600 endpoint licenses, nearly all are deployed on Cisco collaboration room systems to monitor Microsoft Teams and Webex meeting quality. Fewer than 100 licenses are currently dedicated to monitoring the digital experience of our employees.

The proposed investment transforms ThousandEyes from a collaboration monitoring platform into an enterprise-scale Digital Experience Monitoring solution, enabling visibility into the technology experience of nearly half of our workforce.

## Why This Matters

Our monitoring strategy has historically focused on infrastructure health—servers, networks, cloud connectivity, and applications—and has successfully provided visibility into the health of our technology platforms.

However, our ability to understand how technology issues affect employees remains extremely limited.

**Infrastructure monitoring tells us when technology is unhealthy. Digital experience monitoring tells us when people are unable to do their jobs.**

Today, we have meaningful visibility into conference room collaboration systems, but almost no representative visibility into the day-to-day experience of employees using business-critical applications from offices, home networks, and client sites.

As the firm's technology portfolio continues to shift toward SaaS and cloud-hosted platforms, understanding employee experience is becoming as important as understanding infrastructure health.

## Business Benefits

### Expand Employee Experience Visibility

Increase employee-focused monitoring from fewer than 100 monitored users today to as many as 13,000 endpoint agents, providing representative visibility across approximately 50% of the firm's workforce.

### Support All Business Units

Deploy endpoint agents strategically across Tax, Audit, Advisory, and Business Process Group populations to ensure technology decisions are based on representative user experience rather than isolated samples.

### Improve Service Assurance

Move beyond identifying technical failures to understanding business impact by determining who is affected, where they are located, and which services are preventing employees from performing their work.

### Improve SaaS and Cloud Visibility

Measure the real-world performance of critical cloud platforms such as ServiceNow, Microsoft 365, Salesforce, and other SaaS applications from the employee's perspective, rather than relying solely on infrastructure metrics.

### Reduce Time to Resolution

Identify regional ISP issues, office-specific connectivity problems, remote worker issues, and intermittent application degradation significantly faster by correlating infrastructure health with actual employee experience.

### Strengthen Vendor Accountability

Leverage objective end-user performance data to validate service quality, support SLA discussions, and hold cloud providers accountable for the digital experience they deliver.

## Financial Impact

* Current annual spend: Approximately $1.0M
* Proposed annual spend: Approximately $1.5M
* Incremental investment: Approximately $500K annually

The proposed agreement preserves our existing infrastructure monitoring capabilities while increasing employee digital experience visibility by more than two orders of magnitude—from fewer than 100 monitored employees today to as many as 13,000 endpoint agents over the life of the agreement.

## Conclusion

This investment closes one of the largest remaining visibility gaps in the firm's observability strategy.

Our existing deployment effectively monitors collaboration room technology. The proposed expansion extends that same level of visibility to the employees who depend on technology every day to serve our clients.

Rather than simply monitoring whether systems are available, we will be able to understand whether technology is enabling—or preventing—our people from doing their jobs. This capability is foundational to our long-term Service Assurance strategy and will position the firm to better support a growing, increasingly cloud-based, and increasingly hybrid workforce.
