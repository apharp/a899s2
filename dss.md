### IBO: Expand Enterprise Monitoring Capabilities and Visibility

During the year, I significantly expanded the firm's monitoring capabilities, with a particular focus on increasing visibility, improving operational efficiency, and reducing manual effort through automation and standardization.

#### ThousandEyes Expansion and Modernization

I led a major expansion of our ThousandEyes platform, significantly increasing the consistency, coverage, and operational effectiveness of network and user experience monitoring across the firm.

Key accomplishments included:

* Standardized monitoring and testing methodologies across all offices and endpoint agents, creating a consistent monitoring framework that improves data quality and simplifies operational support.
* Designed and implemented new underlay and overlay monitoring capabilities, providing deeper visibility into network performance and end-user experience while reducing reliance on other teams for implementation.
* Developed a fully automated ThousandEyes agent deployment process that allows endpoint agents to be deployed to any firm laptop with a single action, reducing deployment timelines from days or weeks to approximately 15 minutes.
* Implemented ThousandEyes Adaptive Alerting, enabling intelligent baseline learning and significantly reducing alert complexity. This eliminated the need to manually establish and maintain unique thresholds for every office while improving the team's ability to identify meaningful service degradation.
* Established sub-minute monitoring for critical data center and office connectivity, providing near-continuous visibility into key network paths and enabling detection of transient network issues that would previously have gone unnoticed.

These improvements substantially increased our monitoring coverage, accelerated deployment capabilities, reduced operational overhead, and improved our ability to proactively identify and respond to network issues.

#### Monitoring Platform Enhancements and Automation

In addition to expanding ThousandEyes, I led several initiatives to improve the scalability, reliability, and operational maturity of the firm's monitoring ecosystem.

Key accomplishments included:

* Implemented automated device onboarding for SolarWinds, ensuring newly deployed network devices are automatically discovered, monitored, and alerted without manual intervention. This eliminated a long-standing operational gap and improved monitoring consistency across the environment.
* Delivered automated alert suppression integrated with ServiceNow change records. Monitoring systems now automatically suppress alerts for assets associated with approved maintenance windows, significantly reducing manual effort and minimizing unnecessary alert noise for the NOC and support teams.
* Developed a scalable incident auto-routing framework that standardized alert routing logic and reduced administrative complexity while improving maintainability and operational resilience.
* Implemented SquaredUp dashboard automation, empowering operational teams to rapidly create on-demand dashboards for incident response and war room scenarios without requiring engineering support, increasing self-service capabilities across the organization.
* Expanded monitoring coverage into non-production environments, providing teams with greater visibility into application and infrastructure health throughout the full lifecycle rather than limiting visibility to production and disaster recovery systems.
* Instrumented continuous IP SLA monitoring for critical network circuits, enabling rapid detection of service degradation and intermittent connectivity issues.

Collectively, these enhancements improved operational efficiency, increased monitoring coverage, reduced manual effort, and strengthened the firm's ability to proactively manage service health.

#### SolarWinds Replacement Program

One of my key objectives for the year was leading the evaluation and selection of a strategic replacement for the firm's SolarWinds platform.

To achieve this objective, I led a structured evaluation process that included:

* Identification and evaluation of multiple monitoring platforms.
* Development of objective scoring criteria and comparative assessments.
* Hands-on proof-of-concept testing and capability validation.
* Collaboration with stakeholders across technology teams to gather requirements and feedback.
* Presentation of findings and recommendations to leadership.
* Completion of vendor selection and approval processes.

As of the review period, the evaluation phase has been successfully completed, a preferred solution has been selected, and the necessary organizational approvals have been obtained to proceed. The project remains on track to complete procurement and begin implementation during the current fiscal year.

This work positions the firm to modernize a critical monitoring platform while reducing technical debt and improving long-term monitoring capabilities.
